It serves as the guide of the entire project because it identifies the expected outcomes and goals of the research.The objectives aim to:Define the goals of the studyExplain what the project intends to accomplishGuide the development and implementation of the systemServe as basis for evaluation of the project success1.3 Conceptual Framework	A Conceptual Framework is a visual or written representation that shows the relationship among the important concepts, variables, processes, and components of a study or capstone project. It serves as the foundation and guide of the research by illustrating how the proposed system or study will work. In capstone projects, the conceptual framework explains the flow of the study from the inputs, processes, and outputs of the proposed system.Conceptual Framework is based on the System Development Life Cycle (SDLC) that shows how a system is developed step-by-step, from planning up to maintenance. Instead of only Input–Process–Output (IPO), it focuses on the phases of system development.1.4 Scope and Limitation of the Study	Defines what the study covers (scope) and what it does not cover (limitation).
